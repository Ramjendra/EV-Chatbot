SHARP EV — Sample Knowledge Base Document (POC Test Data)

=== BATTERY & RANGE ===

Maximum Range:
The SHARP EV has a maximum range of 400 km (WLTP) on a full charge under normal driving conditions.
Range may vary based on temperature, driving speed, and use of climate control systems.

Battery Capacity:
The vehicle is equipped with a 75 kWh lithium-ion battery pack.
Usable capacity is 72 kWh. The battery management system (BMS) reserves 3 kWh to protect cell longevity.

Charging Time:
- AC Level 2 (7.2 kW): Full charge in approximately 11 hours.
- DC Fast Charging (100 kW): 80% charge in approximately 35 minutes.
- DC Ultra Fast (150 kW): 80% charge in approximately 22 minutes.
Note: Charging speed above 80% is automatically reduced to protect the battery.

=== WARNING LIGHTS ===

Battery Warning Light (Red):
A red battery icon indicates critically low state of charge (below 5%).
Action: Find a charging point immediately. Vehicle range is less than 20 km.

Battery Warning Light (Yellow):
A yellow battery icon indicates low state of charge (below 15%).
Action: Charge the vehicle within the next 50 km.

Motor Temperature Warning:
An orange thermometer icon on the motor indicator means the motor is overheating.
Action: Reduce speed. If the light stays on, pull over safely and allow the vehicle to cool.

Charging Fault (P0AA6):
This code indicates a high-voltage battery isolation fault.
Action: Do not attempt to charge. Contact a SHARP authorised service centre immediately.

=== REGENERATIVE BRAKING ===

Activating Regenerative Braking:
1. Press the REGEN button on the centre console to cycle through modes:
   - OFF: Standard hydraulic braking only.
   - LOW: Light regeneration. Suitable for highway driving.
   - MED: Moderate regeneration. Recommended for city driving.
   - HIGH: Strong regeneration. One-pedal driving possible.

Energy Recovery:
In HIGH mode, up to 150 kW of energy can be recovered during deceleration.
This can extend range by up to 15% in stop-start urban driving.

=== ERROR CODES ===

P0A80 — Replace Hybrid/EV Battery Pack:
This code indicates one or more battery modules have degraded below acceptable levels.
Symptoms: Reduced range, reduced performance, warning light.
Action: Book a diagnostic appointment at a SHARP service centre. Battery replacement may be required.

P1E00 — Electric Motor Control Module:
The motor control unit has detected an internal fault.
Action: Restart the vehicle. If the code reappears, do not drive the vehicle. Tow to service centre.

P0A09 — DC/DC Converter Performance:
The DC/DC converter (which charges the 12V auxiliary battery) is underperforming.
Symptoms: 12V battery warning, infotainment issues.
Action: Schedule service within 1 week. Vehicle is safe to drive short distances.

=== CHARGING GUIDANCE ===

Optimal Charging Practice:
- Keep daily charge level between 20% and 80% for long battery life.
- Only charge to 100% before long trips.
- Avoid charging immediately after a fast-charge session — allow 30 minutes to cool.

Charging in Rain:
The SHARP EV charging port is rated IP55 and is safe to use in rain.
Always ensure the charging cable connector is dry before inserting into the port.

=== TYRE INFORMATION ===

Recommended Tyre Pressure:
- Front: 2.5 bar (36 PSI)
- Rear: 2.7 bar (39 PSI)
For maximum range on long journeys, increase pressure by 0.2 bar.
Check pressure monthly and before long trips.

=== SERVICE INTERVALS ===

Annual Service:
Electric vehicles require less maintenance than petrol/diesel vehicles.
Recommended annual checks:
- Brake fluid (replace every 2 years)
- Cabin air filter (replace every 20,000 km)
- Tyre rotation (every 10,000 km)
- 12V auxiliary battery check (every 3 years)
- Battery health diagnostic (every 30,000 km)

No oil changes required. No timing belt. No spark plugs.
